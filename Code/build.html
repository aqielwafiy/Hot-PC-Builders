GPU

The GPU is the CPU of the graphics card. It is effectively an ASIC or Application Specific Integrated Circuit. Most GPUs run with no or very little cache compared to CPUs because they don’t rely on instruction set queuing. They are designed to process raw number computations and calculations (compute) in the form of floating-point operations and other forms of raw math. This means they calculate the vector angles of 3D scene from the draw calls it makes from the CPU.

There are many reasons for the graphics card shortage, from Covid-19 supply chain issues, to the looming spectre of GPU mining, but that doesn't change the fact we have as much visibility over fresh cards coming into the channel.

For our GPU, the Nvidia GeForce RTX 3080 is one of the best price for performance cards on the market. The RTX 3080 will provide a consistent 60fps on many demanding games like PUBG at Ultra at 1080p, and even 1440p for some games like Apex Legends. It'll also handle Fortnite with zero issues. For those interested in VR, this card will have no problem playing any and and all applications currently on the market.
